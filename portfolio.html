Portfolio

I believe in designing solutions that make an impact. Here are some of my favorite projects that combine creativity, engineering, and robotics. Ask me about the process behind them!

My Projects

Exoskeleton Supporting Shoulder

This wearable exoskeleton project focuses on reducing shoulder strain and enhancing efficiency during repetitive lifting tasks. By redesigning connectors and optimizing joint angles, the system achieves superior structural stability, preventing mechanical conflicts and failure risks. A detailed geometric force analysis informed design choices to mitigate bending moments and shear forces while prioritizing lightweight construction. Future enhancements will integrate a refined control system, including circuit optimization, adaptive algorithms, and motor synchronization for seamless performance.

Stoch 4

Designed and built a robot from scratch, managing the entire process from initial concept to manufacturing and assembly. A custom heatsink design was developed to significantly enhance the robot's thermal performance, improving its runtime through optimization based on real-world data from hardware tests. Additionally, the project included designing the electronics layout and collaborating on the integration of the ROS framework to ensure seamless functionality and advanced operational capabilities.

Rocket Landing Model Predictive Control

Developed and implemented a Model Predictive Control (MPC) framework to simulate the vertical descent of rockets, ensuring precise trajectories and accurate landings. The project involved designing a simplified linear dynamics model to optimize rocket positions, orientation, and thrust inputs during descent. Extensive testing of MPC controllers, including open-loop and closed-loop scenarios with varying horizons, was conducted to evaluate robustness and efficiency. To validate the framework's effectiveness, 3D animations showcasing successful rocket landings were generated. This work emphasizes improving landing accuracy for reusable rocket systems while maintaining computational efficiency.

Stoch-3

Development of quadruped robot designed for agile and dynamic locomotion. Prioritizing lightweight components and structural integrity, the design underwent iterative improvements to shank and foot padding, optimizing materials for enhanced robustness and impact resistance. The integration of foot sensors enabled precise contact detection, critical for navigating uneven terrain and ensuring stable movement. These advancements support the robot’s ability to adapt to complex environments, with applications ranging from exploration to disaster response.

5-DOF Robotic Manipulator

Design and 3D printing of a versatile 5-degree-of-freedom robotic arm. Capable of performing tasks such as span welding, laser cutting, and more, the robotic arm demonstrates adaptability for various industrial applications. Simulations were conducted using RoboDK to validate its functionality and optimize performance

Voice-Operated Robotic Arm

This project involved designing and developing a voice-controlled robotic humanoid arm integrated with IoT for remote operations. The system features a 3D-printed robotic arm with five articulated fingers, each offering three degrees of freedom to replicate human-like gestures. Integration with virtual assistant enables seamless voice command execution, allowing real-time control over the internet without physical range constraints. Emphasizing multifunctionality and reprogrammability, the design prioritizes user-friendly interaction, making it adaptable for diverse applications while maintaining accessibility and ease of use.

Stoch Lite

This project focuses on developing a compact, agile quadruped robot designed to explore advanced control strategies and gait analysis. Stochlite’s small form factor enables efficient testing and iteration of walking trajectories, balance algorithms, and dynamic locomotion patterns. The robot serves as a platform for implementing and refining motion controllers, with a focus on stability, adaptability, and energy efficiency. Key work includes the integration of custom gait generation systems and real-time feedback mechanisms, providing insights into legged robotics fundamentals while prioritizing scalability for future applications in constrained environments.

Robocon

Participated in the ABU Robocon, a robotics competition held in the Asia-Pacific region. Each year, the competition presented unique themes and challenges, requiring us to design and build robots capable of performing specific tasks to compete against other teams. My involvement spanned various aspects of robot development, including conceptualization, design, and implementation, with the goal of achieving competition objectives. This experience honed my skills in teamwork, problem-solving, and innovation in robotics.

Animatronic Head Setup

This final year project focuses on designing and building an animatronic head capable of replicating human-like expressions with precision. The project initially began with developing realistic eye movements, laying the foundation for lifelike facial interactions. The aim is to create a setup that mimics natural facial movements and emotional responses. By integrating mechanical components, controls, and detailed design, the project explores the intersection of robotics and human interaction, paving the way for applications in entertainment, education, and assistive technologies.

Data Collection Box

a specialized device designed to collect detailed terrain information in challenging environments where human access is limited. Equipped with LiDAR, three cameras, GPS, and an IMU, this data collection box captures comprehensive environmental data. Mounted on a mule for ease of mobility in difficult terrains, the device enables seamless data acquisition. The collected data is stitched together to create complete terrain maps, which are further utilized for training and testing quadruped robots. This includes developing training policies and evaluating control strategies to improve robot performance in complex and dynamic environments.

Smart Helmet Accessory

Wearable device designed to enhance safety and communication for construction site workers by retrofitting conventional helmets with IoT-enabled technology. The accessory integrates a Raspberry Pi to enable real-time, hands-free communication between onsite workers and offsite teams, facilitating instant updates, hazard alerts, and coordination. Built to seamlessly attach to standard safety helmets, the system prioritizes durability and user comfort while maintaining compliance with industrial safety standards. By merging cost-effective hardware with intuitive software, the design empowers workers to operate more efficiently in dynamic environments, reducing risks and improving situational awareness.
